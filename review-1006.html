Core Lessons & Takeaways

Five anchors that frame how we prompt, plan, and ship work with agents across Magniteâ€™s operating rhythm.

Lesson 01

Prompt vs Context Engineering

  • Prompts frame objectives, rules, and closing checklists; contexts ensure only relevant history is loaded.
  • Keep chats scoped â€” start a new thread when tackling a separate task to avoid token bloat.
  • Lead with constraints (what to do / not do) and keep language explicit over verbose.
Practical split: Taught concepts in one chat, built the Gong MCP in another â€” cleaner edits and faster resolve.
Lesson 02

RIPER-5 Modes

  • Research gathers context only; Innovate compares options; Plan creates the concrete checklist.
  • Execute applies approved steps; Review checks diffs, acceptance criteria, and regressions.
  • Return to Plan when scope shifts or gaps surface mid-execution.
Live checklist: Declare mode, forbid edits until Plan is ready, execute only approved steps, log with emojis.
Lesson 03

Cursor Chat vs Codex

  • Cursor excels at tight loops, debugging, and research.
  • Codex handles multi-file edits and longer tasks with explicit plans plus repo context.
  • Switch when a job will span 10+ minutes or touch multiple files â€” codify the rules first.
Lesson 04

Project Scaffolding Patterns

  • Pair agents.md, plans, and emoji logs for every significant task.
  • Keep secrets in .env; scope MCP configs per project to control exposure.
  • Compose a few general-purpose tools instead of proliferating narrow ones.
Lesson 05

Communication & UI Patterns

  • Lean on Magnite gradients, card lift, and motion for branded decks.
  • Use slide-as-card sections with phase badges and concise, high-contrast copy.

Working Rituals

  • Monitor token usage; split tasks when chats creep past the ~50% context mark.
  • Front-load goals and rules, close with a checklist of deliverables.
  • Separate research, plan, and execute turns to prevent chaotic edits.
  • Keep logs readable: emoji prefix, one line per event.

Risks & Mitigations

Edge cases we identified during the build and how to keep them contained as the workflow scales.

Primary Risks

  • Empty transcripts: Favor summary-first flows and flag derived vs primary sources.
  • Rate limits: Throttle/batch large syncs; use pagination and backoff.
  • Ambiguity in terms: Maintain glossary + disambiguation examples inside prompts.
  • Overgrown context: Split work into fresh chats when scope shifts.
  • Runaway edits: Enforce mode discipline and plan checkpoints.
  • Secrets exposure: Keep .env in .gitignore; never paste secrets in chat.

Mitigation Moves

  • Build summary-first briefs, then share transcripts only when needed.
  • Queue nightly jobs + webhook or poll for incrementals.
  • Seed prompts with product dictionaries and role-based examples.
  • Re-seed chats with just the relevant files before long turns.
  • Require plan checklists before entering Execute on sizable edits.
  • Verify environment configs locally and restrict secrets to runtime.
